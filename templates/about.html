About This Project

A simple BTech 2nd Year project on health risk prediction

Project Overview

The Early Health Detection system is a comprehensive health assessment tool that offers two main functionalities:

Health Risk Assessment

Predicts whether a person has a low, medium, or high health risk based on their health metrics including:

  • Age and gender
  • Heart rate and blood pressure
  • Cholesterol and blood sugar levels
  • BMI and lifestyle factors
  • Family history of diseases
Disease Prediction

Identifies possible diseases based on symptoms reported by the user. The system can detect 41 different diseases including:

  • Fungal infections, Allergies, GERD
  • Diabetes, Asthma, Hypertension
  • Malaria, Dengue, Tuberculosis
  • Common Cold, Pneumonia, Heart attack
  • And many more conditions

Machine Learning Algorithms

Our system implements multiple machine learning algorithms to provide accurate predictions:

Naive Bayes

A probabilistic classifier based on Bayes' theorem. It's called "naive" because it assumes all features are independent of each other.

Bayes' Theorem:

P(A|B) = P(B|A) Ã— P(A) / P(B)

Works well for disease classification with many features.

Decision Tree

Creates a tree-like model of decisions based on feature values. Each internal node represents a test on a feature, each branch represents an outcome of the test, and each leaf node represents a class label.

Provides easily interpretable results and can handle both numerical and categorical data.

Random Forest

An ensemble learning method that constructs multiple decision trees during training and outputs the class that is the mode of the classes of the individual trees.

Reduces overfitting and provides higher accuracy than individual decision trees.

Gradient Boosting

A machine learning technique that produces a prediction model in the form of an ensemble of weak prediction models, typically decision trees.

Builds models sequentially, with each new model correcting errors made by previously trained models.

How It Works

Step 1: Data Collection

Users input their health metrics through a form:

  • Age and gender
  • Heart rate and blood pressure
  • Cholesterol and blood sugar
  • BMI and lifestyle factors
  • Family history of diseases

The system validates the inputs and prepares them for analysis.

Step 2: Risk Analysis

The system processes the health metrics to determine risk level:

  1. Data is preprocessed and normalized
  2. The model calculates the probability of each risk level (low, medium, high)
  3. Risk factors are identified and weighted
  4. A comprehensive risk score is generated
Step 3: Results Visualization

The system presents the results in an easy-to-understand format:

Risk Category

Clear indication of whether the risk is low, medium, or high

Contributing Factors

Visualization of which factors contribute most to the risk

Recommendations

Suggestions for improving health metrics and reducing risk

Step 1: Symptom Selection

Users select their symptoms from categorized lists:

  • Skin symptoms (rash, itching, etc.)
  • Respiratory symptoms (cough, breathlessness, etc.)
  • Digestive symptoms (nausea, vomiting, etc.)
  • Pain and discomfort symptoms
  • General symptoms (fever, fatigue, etc.)

Users can also search for specific symptoms and select an ML algorithm.

Step 2: Multi-Algorithm Analysis

The system processes the symptoms using multiple ML algorithms:

  1. Symptoms are encoded as binary features (present/absent)
  2. Selected algorithm (or all algorithms) analyze the symptom pattern
  3. Each algorithm calculates probability scores for possible diseases
  4. Results are compared across algorithms for consensus
Step 3: Comprehensive Results

The system presents detailed disease prediction results:

Most Likely Condition

Primary prediction with confidence score

Contributing Symptoms

Which symptoms were most significant in the prediction

Algorithm Comparison

How different ML algorithms performed on the same symptoms
