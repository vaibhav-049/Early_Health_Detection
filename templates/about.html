About This Project

A simple BTech 2nd Year project on health risk prediction

Project Overview

This is a simple health risk prediction system that uses the Naive Bayes machine learning algorithm to predict whether a person has a low, medium, or high health risk based on their health metrics.

What is Naive Bayes?

Naive Bayes is a simple machine learning algorithm based on Bayes' theorem. It's called "naive" because it assumes all features are independent of each other (which isn't usually true in real life, but the algorithm still works well for many problems).

Bayes' Theorem:

P(A|B) = P(B|A) Ã— P(A) / P(B)

The algorithm learns from our sample health data and then uses that learning to predict health risks for new inputs.

How It Works

Step 1: Training

The model is trained using a sample dataset with health metrics like:

  • Age and gender
  • Heart rate and blood pressure
  • Cholesterol and blood sugar
  • BMI and lifestyle factors

Each record in our dataset has a known risk level (low, medium, or high).

Step 2: Prediction

When you enter your health information:

  1. Your data is sent to our Flask server
  2. The Naive Bayes model calculates the probability of each risk level
  3. The model assigns the most likely risk level to your data
  4. You get a visual report of your health risk
Important: This is a student project created for educational purposes. The predictions are not medically validated and should not be used for real health decisions.
